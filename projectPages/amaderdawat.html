Amader Dawat

Organizer Role

As a lead organizer of Amader Dawat (transl: "Our Gathering") I contributed heavily to conversations surrounding event planning and strategies. In addition to planning fundraising and community events alongside a small team of other organizers, I primarily spearheaded the social media marketing and brand content. This meant evolving Amader Dawat's existing brand identity to a more sophisticated and consistent style, managing communications through Instagram, and creating eye-catching graphics to promote Amader Dawat's efforts. Joining the team at such an early stage of this organization's existence and after a brief hiatus from the founding organizers, I was facing the challenge of building momentum after months of sparse activity for programming with which I had vey little familiarity.


Style Guide

In an effort to create more consistent branding that could be carried forward even without my direct contribution to marketing, I first had to establish a visual identity. I created a visual identity whiteboard, which held the primary purpose of very clearly communicating visual styling and hierarchy as knowledge that could passed forward long after my volunteership. This whiteboard features fonts used for styling header and paragraph content, recurring graphics for specific events, an array of colors used for general social media posts and promoting monthly programming, and an array of post backgrounds serving as example for marketing larger events. Using this board, I could quickly reference significant design elements and piece together a cohesive visual identity.

Specifications

  • Previously established logo with significant emphasis on balance of languages
  • Sans-serif, handwriting-esque fonts as headings for a friendly and approachable first impression
  • Polychromatic colorboard based on colors appearing in the logo, as well as recurring colors in existing marketing that are appropriate complements
  • Gradients in complementary pink and green tones for the energy of compassion, playfulness, and growth

Marketing Impact

Amader Dawat's main focus is on community building through event programming, so the majority of social media content involves promotion of different-sized events. These events draw in the common audience of queer Bengali folks, though some events are specified to be closed to the community or open to allies. Amader Dawat aims to create an intergenerational safe space for queer Bengali creators and artists to be celebrated and find support within the community.

Signature Events

  • Amader Robibar (Our Sunday), a small event recurring monthly to bring LGBTQ+ Bengali folks in NYC together in one room to share personal experiences and provide resources.
  • Pohela Boishakh (Bengali New Year), Amader Dawat's flagship event to celebrate Bengali culture and uplift artists within the community

Within my first month of joining as an organizer, Amader Dawat's social media has had a 350% increase in profile activity and a 700% increase in reaching new accounts. In total my fundraising efforts have led to more than $7000 in community donations.

Open Mic Teaser

One of the first large events I worked on marketing for was a community open mic, with a featured drag performer, resources provided by various other grassroots organizations, and audiovisual equipment for anyone to share their art. This event was to serve as a soft-launch fundraiser to build momentum for Amader Dawat and raise money for a large event due to take place mid-2026. However, due to limited volunteer capacity and budgeting concerns, details for the event would be finalized last minute. Therefore, to build hype for the event with this significant delay, I created a teaser trailer. The trailer was created in a way to excite audiences for what's to come without commitment to a set time and location, offering just enough details to support marketing when event specifications were not yet finalized.

Reflections

There is still plenty of work to be done as Amader Dawat is still a small organization at the beginning stages of their activism. As a queer Bengali person myself, I find this to be such a valuable volunteer experience, and I wish this organization existed while I was growing up. Through our efforts, we hope to connect our queer elders, motivate young adult audiences, and provide inspiration for queer Bengali youth today.
